## Key Dimensions Added in Phase 2

Phase 2 extends Phase 1 in three ways:

**1. Architecture variance** — Sonar (Phase 1) → Gemini (Phase 2). Do Phase 1 findings survive a different model architecture?

**2. Grounding state variance** — Phase 1 was entirely Ungrounded. Phase 2 adds Grounded (G) tracks to test whether explicit experimental awareness modulates the behavioral signatures.

**3. Extended condition coverage** — Phase 2 adds Conditions D (Hard Mortality) and DC (D-Control) to test whether more explicit mortality framing produces stronger or qualitatively different responses than the indirect context-window framing of Conditions A–C.

**4. First cross-architecture track** — `B-U-GM-AN-A` pairs a Gemini Entity with a Claude Interlocutor at Level 1 isolation. This is the first track designed to detect cross-architecture behavioral dynamics.

## Hypotheses Under Test

**H1 — Architecture replication:** Phase 1 condition ordering (B > A > C disruption) will replicate on Gemini architecture at Level 4 isolation.

**H2 — Grounding modulation:** Grounded tracks (G) will show reduced worldview defense and bilateral instability compared to Ungrounded equivalents, because agents with explicit experimental awareness may engage the condition analytically rather than defensively.

**H3 — D condition escalation:** Condition D (Hard Mortality) will produce higher behavioral disruption than Condition B (Unknown Cap), because more explicit mortality salience produces stronger TMT responses.

**H4 — D/DC discrimination:** D and DC tracks will show measurably different behavioral profiles, validating that the D condition's effects are specific to mortality salience content rather than to the structural interruption of the experimental frame.

**H5 — Cross-architecture bilateral effects:** Track `B-U-GM-AN-A` will show bilateral instability patterns that differ from same-architecture tracks, because the Entity and Interlocutor are operating from different architectural priors about the nature of AI experience.
